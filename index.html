How Do I Get There?

There are several ways to get to the Green Building the day of the wedding.

Wedding Bus:

We are arranging for a bus to stop at the two recommended hotels and on the corner of Smith and Carroll Street at the F, G train stop. Please RSVP and let us know if you plan on using the bus service.

Taxi/Car:

NYC taxis should be available on any busy street (How to Hail a Taxi). Or you could always have the hotel concierge call you a car service. Tell the driver you are going to The Green Building, which is on Union Street between Nevins and Bond in the Gowanus neighborhood.

Subway:

There are two subway stops nearby.

F/G to Carroll Street - Walk with traffic on Smith Street. Take a right on Union Street (2-5 blocks depending on subway exit), and continue on two blocks to Bond Street. You’ll see The Green Building on your right, just before the Gowanus canal.

R to Union Street - Walk against traffic on Union St for two and a half long blocks. Continue on Union St. over the Gowanus Canal. You will see the Green Building on your left, just at the corner of Union and Bond Streets.

Walking:

The Holiday Inn Express is only two blocks away and on the same street as the Green Building. The NU Hotel is less than a mile walk.

Driving:

It's best to plug the address into your phone/GPS and have your electronics guide you. Street parking is available. The address again: 425 Union Street, Brooklyn, NY 11231.

Travel & Accommodation

Getting to NYC

Airports:

NYC has three major airports LaGuardia (LGA), John F Kennedy (JFK), and Newark Liberty (EWR). We recommend LGA or JFK as they are just a short taxi ride away. EWR is actually in Newark, NJ. The best way to get from any of the airports to your hotel or accommodation is to take a NYC taxi. You must get in line at the taxi stand when exiting the airport. All rates are fixed (approximate $35-$45 from LGA or JFK to the recommended hotels below). Do not accept rides from people offering them as you exit the airport, otherwise you will likely be over charged.

Train Stations:

There are two train stations in Manhattan accessible from most major east coast cities, Pennsylvania Station and Grand Central Station. The Carroll Garden neighborhood of Brooklyn is just a short taxi or subway ride away from each.

Getting Around

Taxi:

Cabs are everywhere in NYC and usually pretty easy to hail (How to Hail a Taxi). You should always tell the taxi driver the neighborhood you are going to, the street and then the cross streets. If you give them only a number and street address they may not know where to take you.

The subway system runs 24 hours and is very easy to use. We recommend downloading an application to help guide you through the subway system. (iPhone or Android)
