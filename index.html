Travel & Accommodation

Getting to NYC

Airports:

NYC has three major airports LaGuardia (LGA), John F Kennedy (JFK), and Newark Liberty (EWR). We recommend LGA or JFK as they are just a short taxi ride away. EWR is actually in Newark, NJ.

The best way to get from any of the airports to your hotel or accommodation is to take a NYC taxi. You must get in line at the taxi stand when exiting the airport. All rates are fixed (approximate $35-$45 from LGA or JFK to the recommended hotels below). Do not accept rides from people offering them as you exit the airport, otherwise you will likely be over charged.

Train Stations:

There are two train stations in Manhattan accessible from most major east coast cities, Pennsylvania Station and Grand Central Station. The Carroll Garden neighborhood of Brooklyn is just a short taxi or subway ride away from each.

Getting Around

Taxi:

Cabs are everywhere in NYC and usually pretty easy to hail (How to Hail a Taxi). You should always tell the taxi driver the neighborhood you are going to, the street and then the cross streets. If you give them only a number and street address they may not know where to take you.

Subway:

The subway system runs 24 hours and is very easy to use. We recommend downloading an application to help guide you through the subway system. (iPhone or Android)

Car Service:

Things to Do

Things to do in Brooklyn near the Wedding Venue and hotels

Explore Smith St and Court Street: Grab a drink at Clover Club its one of our favorites.


Brooklyn Bridge Park: You can see the NYC skyline, walk across the bridge, take a water taxi, ride the carousel, or explore Dumbo. On the weekend they usually have an open food fair in the park too. If you love crowds and waiting in line grab some pizza at the famous Grimaldi’s.


Prospect Park: Snap a pic at Grand Army Plaza, walk around the park, see the Botanical Gardens.


Barclays Center: there are always cool things going on at this brand new brooklyn stadium; music, shows, sports.

Things to do Midtown Manhattan

Splurge on a Broadway show or go to the TKTS booth to get discounted theater tickets. We suggest Nook restaurant if you you are going to the theater but please note it’s byob and only good for small groups.


If you have never been to Time Square this is our suggestion... walk to the center of Time Square, look up, do a 360 degree turn, snap a picture, then immediately leave Time Square.


New York City Public Library and Bryant Park.

Chinatown and Financial District

Get some noodles at Tasty Hand Pulled Noodles. Then grab a drink at Apotheke which is a few doorways down. It’s a Speakeasy so there is no sign, just a big door with someone standing outside.


Ride the Staten Island Ferry: It’s free, they serve drinks, and its a great way to go on a water “cruise” to see the Statue of Liberty.

Lower East Side and the East Village

Jeepney: Amazing Filipino gastropub in the East Village.


The Tenement Museum does excellent walking tours of the Lower East Side while focusing on buildings or urban immigrant history.

Williamsburg Brooklyn (this is where we live!)

Smorgasburg is a food flea market along the river. You can eat all kinds of delicious foods and snacks and buy cool crafts and goods.


If you are in the mood for awesome steak skip Peter Lugers and instead go to St. Anselm.


If you want some awesome American style barbecue Fete Sau is the place to go.


If you are in town for a few days and looking for something cool to do maybe take a class at Brooklyn Kitchen. They have chefs from some of NYC’s top restaurants come in and teach 2 hours classes. You get to eat all the food and they usually do a wine or beer pairing.


Looking for a cool view. Grab a drink from the bar at the top of the The Ides @ The Wythe Hotel.

Bronx & Queens

See the Yankees play in the Bronx. Bleachers seats are usually available and pretty cheap. Or if you prefer to Mets head out to Queens.
